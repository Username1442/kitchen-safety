Kitchen and Food Safety Tips

Cuts:

  • Sharp knives are safer than dull knives because you use less force.
  • Don’t put knives in soapy water because you can’t see them and might hurt yourself.

Burns:

  • Don’t let water get into your oven mitts because water conducts heat.
  • Don’t put metal in the microwave because it could get very hot.
  • For minor burns, rinse under cold water to stabilize blood vessels.

Fire:

  • Don't put water on grease fires as it only makes it worse.
  • Baking soda can help put out fires.
  • When using a fire extinguisher, aim at the base of the fire.

Electrocution:

  • Don't put water near cords as an electric shock can easily happen.
  • When unplugging a cord, don’t pull the cord as it can lead to electric shock.
  • If something is stuck in a toaster, don’t use a metal object to pull it out.

Falls:

  • Clean up spills immediately to prevent falls.
  • Keep drawers closed when not in use to prevent tripping up on them.

Foodborne Illness:

  • Don’t leave meat out for longer than 2 hours to prevent bacteria growing.
  • Thaw frozen food in a refrigerator to prevent bacteria growing.
